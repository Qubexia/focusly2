## Design Context

### Users
High school and university students use Zakerly to organize their full study life — subjects, weekly schedules, pomodoro focus sessions, streaks, exams/tasks/revisions, and AI-generated study packs from lecture notes. They open the app in short bursts between classes and in longer dedicated study blocks. Premium unlocks unlimited subjects, full analytics, AI notes, and priority reminders. The primary surface is the Flutter mobile app (iOS + Android); a separate React admin dashboard serves internal ops, not students.

### Brand Personality
Modern, focused, premium.
The interface should evoke **calm focus** — clarity and quiet confidence without noise, urgency, or gamified pressure. Sharp and trustworthy, not loud or playful. Students should feel they are in a precise, well-built study tool, not a generic productivity widget or a meme-heavy student app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Lean **technical** over editorial-soft: precise layouts, structured information density, clean data surfaces, and restrained motion. Zakerly-aligned blue identity (`#0088FF` primary). Material 3 with **both light and dark themes** (system default). Layered blue surfaces, subtle gradients, zero-elevation bordered cards (28px radius), pill buttons (30px radius), and a floating glass bottom nav. Typography: **Outfit** for headings and labels, **Plus Jakarta Sans** for body copy — tight headline tracking, generous line height on body text. Motion via `flutter_animate`: purposeful and functional, never decorative. AI and study content should read as structured, scannable output — not raw API dumps or chat-style bubbles.

The app must be **fully multilingual**: localized UI strings, locale-aware formatting, and proper RTL layout for Arabic (and other RTL locales) across the entire app — not only in AI-generated content.

**Anti-references:** cluttered dashboards, default flat Material chrome, neon/gamified streak UI, generic ChatGPT-style chat bubbles for study content.

### Design Principles
1. **Calm focus first** — every screen should reduce cognitive load and support sustained attention, not hype or urgency.
2. Make study content feel curated and structured, not dumped — especially AI artifacts (summaries, flashcards, questions).
3. Favor a technical, precise visual language: clear hierarchy, scannable data, restrained decoration.
4. Keep interactions obvious and lightweight so students can move fast between schedule → focus → review.
5. Build for **full multilingual support** — localized copy, RTL/LTR layout, and locale-aware content direction throughout the app.
6. Focus and streak features should motivate progress without inducing anxiety or shame.
7. Premium gating should be clear and honest — show value before asking, never dark-pattern the upgrade.
